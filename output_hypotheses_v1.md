2. **Entropy as a Nucleation Biomarker**: Applying Benford’s Law and Shannon entropy to continuous ambient seismic noise to mathematically pinpoint the "nucleation phase"—the exact transition from elastic creep to catastrophic brittle failure—prior to any traditional STA/LTA threshold being triggered.
3. **Predictive SPH-Seismic Coupling**: Developing 3D Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) runout models that are dynamically constrained and updated in real-time by ambient noise interferometry, predicting the exact inundation zone *before* a slope collapses.
4. **Anthropogenic Probes for Slope Stability**: Utilizing daily, predictable human activities (e.g., highway traffic, diurnal reservoir filling) as active seismic sources to monitor temporal velocity variations ($dV/V$) in surrounding unstable rock masses, turning "noise" into an active geotechnical probe.
5. **Microseismic Plane Mapping via Unsupervised Learning**: Leveraging Deep Embedded Clustering (DEC) and advanced autoencoders to blindly separate and track pre-failure microseismicity in real-time, effectively tracing the 3D evolution of a sliding plane before a macroscopic rupture occurs.
6. **Machine Learning XAI for Source Physics**: Transitioning from observational CNN/YOLO detection to Explainable AI (XAI) models capable of differentiating the physical source mechanisms (e.g., differentiating internal pore fluid pressure spikes from structural rock bridge failures) directly from raw waveforms.
7. **Hydrological Control via Velocity Drops**: Using short-term (hourly resolution) seismic velocity drops to quantitatively measure pore pressure spikes and internal hydrology changes inside landslide bodies during intense, extreme, or multi-day rainfall events.
8. **Real-time Inversion of 3D Debris Flow Entrainment**: Utilizing localized long-period seismic inversion to dynamically calculate the rate of mass entrainment and volumetric bulking of debris flows as they actively propagate down a channel system.
9. **Urban Lithosphere Hazard Cascades**: Monitoring how transient, extreme short-duration urban flood events cause rapid decoupling of hillslopes from river channels, immediately reactivating dormant landslide complexes positioned above urban infrastructure.
10. **Quantifying Fragmentation via Rockfall Seismology**: Analyzing high-frequency seismic energy during free-fall rock impacts to quantify the exact physical reduction of potential energy caused by the in-flight disintegration and fragmentation of the rock mass.
